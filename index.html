Quick bio

Hello ! I am a 1st year PhD student in Machine Learning at the Ecole Normale Supérieure de Lyon under the supervision of Mathurin Massias , Titouan Vayer and Paulo Gonçalves. I am a part of OCKHAM, a joint team of Inria1 and CNRS2, led by Rémi Gribonval. My current research interests revolve around optimization for machine learning and high-dimensional statistics. I like open science.

Previously, I was a research intern in Deep Learning in the NeuroAI team of CERCO, a CNRS lab in Toulouse3, under the supervision of Andrea Alamia and Rufin VanRullen, where I had the opportunity to work on the oscillatory dynamics of artificial neural networks under some biological feasibility constraints.

Before that, we may have met in Berlin, Montpellier, Lyon, Istanbul, Beyrouth, Amman or Cairo. Hit me up if that's the case !

Outside of research, I enjoy hiking, running, boxing, food, beers, and "la belle vie" with my girlfriend.

Research

I am currently working on large-scale optimization for statistical machine learning in the context of sparse graph inference. More specifically, on improvements of the estimation of sparse precision matrices, which model conditional (in)dependance relationships between covariates.
I have a particular interest in applications to neuroscience problems.

Broadly speaking, I am interested in optimization theory and its applications to machine learning, high-dimensional statistics, deep learning and computational neuroscience.
